# Netflix Content & Movie Duration Trends Analysis 🎬

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** of the Netflix dataset. The primary goal was to investigate how content characteristics — specifically movie runtimes and genres — have evolved over the decades. 

By analyzing over 80 years of cinematic data, this project translates raw data into strategic business insights, demonstrating how historical shifts, industry economics, and changing consumer habits influence the content strategy of streaming giants like Netflix today.

---

## 💡 Key Insights
Our analysis of average movie duration dynamics from **1940 to 2021** revealed two contrasting historical eras:

* **1940–1965: The Era of Cinematic Scale (Growth)** Average runtimes experienced a sharp, steady increase, peaking around 1965. As the film industry fought for audience attention against the rapid rise of home television, movie studios went "big," producing epic, multi-hour theatrical masterpieces to justify ticket sales.
    
* **Post-1965: The Shift to Dynamic Content (Decline)** Following the mid-60s, average movie durations gradually declined by **20-30%**, eventually stabilizing into a modern "sweet spot" of **95–105 minutes**. 

### 💼 Business Impact & Recommendation:
Modern audiences strongly prefer more fast-paced, dynamic content due to evolving consumption habits and shorter attention spans. For streaming platforms, aligning new original productions (**Netflix Originals**) with a **95-105 minute target runtime** is highly strategic. It optimizes the **completion rate** (the percentage of viewers who watch a film to the end) — which is a critical product metric directly tied to subscriber retention and overall platform engagement.
